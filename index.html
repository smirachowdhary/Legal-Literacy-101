Our Mission

Legal Literacy 101 is dedicated to increasing access to legal knowledge through clear, accurate, and multilingual educational resources. We aim to empower immigrants, workers, and students by explaining their rights in plain language and connecting them to trusted legal information.

Immigrants’ Rights Toolkit

Part 1: If You Are Stopped

  • You have the right to remain silent.
  • You do not have to answer questions about your immigration status.
  • You can ask to speak to a lawyer.
  • You do not have to consent to searches.

What to say:
“I choose to remain silent.”
“I want to speak with a lawyer.”

Part 2: At Work or School

  • Workers have the right to be paid, regardless of immigration status.
  • You can report unsafe or unfair working conditions.
  • Schools cannot deny education based on immigration status.
  • You do not have to disclose your status to employers or schools.

Part 3: Documents & Emergency Planning

  • Carry only required identification.
  • Keep copies of important documents in a safe place.
  • Save contact information for a lawyer or legal aid organization.
  • Have a family emergency plan.
