About

Legal Literacy 101 exists to make the law clear, accessible, and usable.

Our mission

We provide plain-language, multilingual legal information so immigrants, workers, and students understand their rights and feel empowered to advocate for themselves. We believe that access to legal knowledge should not depend on income, language, or background. Everyone deserves to understand the rules that affect their lives.

Want to contribute?

Visit Join the Team or reach out with ideas and topic requests. If you have suggestions for languages, accessibility, or toolkits we should prioritize, we’d love to hear them.

CEO

Smira Chowdhary is a tenth grader at Eastlake High School who enjoys reading, swimming, and spending time with friends. She especially loves math, and some of her favorite book series include The Land of Stories, Fablehaven, and The Prison Healer. Smira founded Legal Literacy 101 to help make legal information clearer and more accessible—especially for immigrants and others who may not know where to start. She is excited to turn complicated legal ideas into plain-language toolkits that people can actually use, and to build a project that supports the community. Through Legal Literacy 101, she also hopes to gain leadership experience by learning how to organize a team, manage projects, and create resources that make a real difference.
